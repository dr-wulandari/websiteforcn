A little about me...

A woman who is - dare I say - almost 40 years old. I have two kids (13 & 10) and I originally came from Indonesia. I have been in UK for a while though, I have worked and studied here (in that order).
My industry was telecommunication, into which I begrudgingly tripped and fell (around 15 years ago). Due to various reasons (IR35, Brexit, and the Covid-19 Pandemic) I found myself redundant, and with this drastic change I resolved to get myself out of the telecommunication industry.
My situation was peculiar, I was not entitled to any help from the UK government because I had been earning well enough and I paid taxes and NI and I had some savings. I went into a Job Centre, and the Manchester City Hall, asking for help as I couldn't afford my £1000 pcm house-rent and I was on the brink of eviction. Their answers were the same: I have to live on my savings until it's all gone and that I am (and my children are) homeless.
It all felt very unfair at the time... if I had never bothered to work, I could probably get government help fairly easily (even housing). But because I did, they wouldn't help until I find myself (and my children) in an actual ditch.

One day, on a financial-forum on the internet, an Independent Financial Advisor made a passing comment, "Just spend all your money then go back to Job Centre."
So I did. I spent everything I had to buy a small terrace house. I took a small mortgage to top it up. Now my mortgage is about £365 pcm which is a lot less than the previous £1000 pcm for a roof over our heads. I had some breathing space.
Armed with zero savings and a newly purchased home, I went back to the Job Centre, and this time around, they processed my application for help. Apparently I am entitled to some £1300 per month, but after various (and mysterious) deductions, Universal Credit commited to give me £250. Yay! Better than nothing. I am grateful.

It was literally on the first day ever of my UC payment that someone directed me to a coding course with Code Nation. That..., brought us here, on this silly website I made myself using only HTML and CSS. I have made websites in the past, but I used Dreamweaver, this time around, I know better. Who would've thought that? At this age, I can still be better.
